## Context

These essays are not blog posts. They are **positioning evidence** — third-party-verifiable proof that the AI and leadership claims on the rest of the site are real. Every piece either reinforces Chris's positioning as a Head of Design candidate or shouldn't be on the site. Five strong pieces beat fifteen mixed ones.

**Who reads these:** executive recruiters, hiring managers, CXOs evaluating Chris as a design leadership hire. They scan fast. The writing needs to be tight, declarative, and grounded in specific work — not thought-leadership fluff.

**Voice:** First person. Confident but not boastful. Specific over abstract. Sentences over marketing copy. The same voice as the case studies on the site — editorial, restrained, precise.

**Length guidance:** 800–1,500 words each. Long enough to be substantive, short enough that a busy recruiter finishes it. The anchor essays (pieces 3 and 5) can run longer if the material demands it.

**Formatting:** Use markdown. `## Headings` for sections. No bullet-point listicles — these are essays, not frameworks. A few bold phrases for scanners are fine. Keep paragraphs short (3–5 sentences).
